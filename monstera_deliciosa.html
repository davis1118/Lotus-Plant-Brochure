Monstera Deliciosa

Monstera deliciosa is a plant native to the rainforests of Central America, also known as the "split-leaf philodendron." One of the most common house plants, it can grow to massive size if cared for in the right conditions. This plant is also known for its hardiness and often found growing in several different lighting conditions.


When growing this plant indoors, you can expect your plant to grow about 1 to 2 feet per year. You can expect your plant to throw out a leaf every few months. The leaf would look glossy and delicate the first couple of days then you will start to notice it become a darker green and stiffen into a matte color. You will start to notice when your plant matures aerial root, which serves the purpose of supporting the large leaves.


When planting outside in the right area, you might notice the plant producing a flower which will eventually turn into a fruit. Plant owner who have tasted this fruit describe the flavor to be a combination of pineapple and banana.


Common Names Split-leaf philodendron, Swiss cheese plant, Ceriman Botanical Name Monstera deliciosa
Botanical Name Monstera deliciosa Family Araceae Plant Type Climbing evergreen Mature Size 3 ft. tall, 2-3 ft wide Sunlight Bright indirect sunlight, partial shade Soil Type Peat-based potting soil, well-drained, aroid soil Soil pH Acidic or neutral Bloom Time Mid-summer (Mostly outside) Native Area Central America Pet Safe? Toxic to cats and dogs




How to care for your Monstera Deliciosa



Monstera deliciosa thrives all year-round when placed in warm, humid environment. When planting it outdoors, make sure the plant in located in part-shade and in well draining soil. Apply a balanced fertilizer about twice a year and skip fertilizing during winter months.



Light
This plant prefers bright and indirect sunlight and constant temperatures between 65 to 75 Fahrenheit. When this plant is exposed in too much direct sunlight the foliage may turn brown and crispy. This is a sign to move it to a shadier part of your home. Many plant owner of monsteras have claimed that the more bright and indirect sunlight the plant receive the more characteristic holes the plant will throw out.


Water
Give this plant regular waterings during the growing season. The soil should dry out between waterings. Frequent waterings should only be given to plants that are in very bright indirect light. Even so it should never be more than twice a week. Wet soil without drying out can cause root rot which can lead to pests which as fungus gnats.


Soil
When this plant in established in a pot, it will require peat-based potting material. Outdoor monsteras can be planted in light sandy, medium loamy, and heavy clay soils. Make sure the soil is acidic or netural is the pH. Monsteras require well-drained soil, that holds a medium amount of moisture.
