Monstera Adasonii

The monstera adansonii is a plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America, also known as "swiss cheese plant." Similar to its cousin the monstera deliciosa, monstera adasonii has a fast growth rate. It has a natural vining habit, typically found growing up trees in the wild.


With the right conditions, the plant can grow up to 3–8 ft. tall indoors and 1–3 ft. wide. You can expect your plant to throw out a leaf every few months. The leaf will be a lighter green and furled up for the first couple of days. Once it unfurls, the leaf color will tend to mature to a darker green. Aerial roots may grow from the nodes of the plant, which serves the purpose of climbing and supporting the large leaves.


When planting outside in the right area, the plant can reach up to 10–13 ft. tall. They may also bloom during Spring, producing white flowers. This plant typically does not flower indoors.





How to care for your new Monstera Adasonii

Light
Placing your monstera adasonii within range of bright to medium sunglight allows this species to flourish at its highest potential.

Water
This plant simply requires to be watered once every week. It is best to wait until the soil has dried 1-2 inches before the next watering session.

Soil
Monsteras perform well in a potting mix that retains moisture but also drains well. They prefer slightly more acidic pH soil, from 5.5-6.5. A soil mix containing 1 part peat moss/coco coir, 1 part perlite, and 4 parts pine bark fines is an excellent mix for Monsteras
