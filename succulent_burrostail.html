Succulent

Burrotail

Often confused with being a cactus, the Burrotail is very much so a succulent. this plant is native to southern Mexico and gets its name from a reference to to donkey's tail.


The Burrotail is a sustainable plant, capable of enduring heat as well as droughts rather well. this allows the plant to survive areas that hotter than usual.


The appearance of the Burro tail is mainly green, however it is common to see variations of this such as blue-green as well as gray-green. This succulent is rather unique as instead of the average looking succulent the Burrotail looks as if it is a rope grown from plants. to play on top of this, it can be showcased in a hanging pot to give your room a nice collection of natural hanging vine like effect.





How to care for your new Burro Tail

Light
The burrotail flourishes when grown indoors. A large source of sunlight is also reccomended for the burrotail. However, it should start with a small amount of sunlight and gradually be exposed to more when first purchasing the succulent.

Water
As for watering, ensure that the soil is dry then water once a month.

Soil
Please keep this succulent within a container that provides ample drainage.
