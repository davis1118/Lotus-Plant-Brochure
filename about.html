Are you watering too much?

Most new plant parents are actually overwatering their plants. April 7, 2021

So you got a new plant and you're ready to start showering it with love and care. After a couple of weeks, you start to notice that its slowly dying on you. The leaves are turning yellow and some are even drooping. Do not worry, this is a common occurence! This is most likely a sign of overwatering. A helpful tip is to stick your finger in the soil about 1 or 2 inches, depending on the plant. If it is dry, there will be little to no dirt on your finger. If it is still moist, then hold off on watering. Overwatering your plant can lead to root rot and nasty pests such as fungus gnats. These problems can be easily avoided and it's best to take precautions. It can also be done with the use of a chopstick, or even a moisture meter. While it is good to shower your plants with care, too much of anything can be damaging. Good luck on your plant journey!

Want more of the same plant for free?

Propagating your plants can help you get more plants without spending a single dime, March 28, 2021

Some plants can be easily propagated through the process of cutting them at specific nodes. Plants such as pothos can be clip at their nodes ,the area just below the aerial roots, and place this cutting into some water. Make sure the knife or clippers you are using are clean to prevent infections. Place these cuttings in bright indirect light and replace water about once a week. Some plants such as peperomia can be propagated at the stem in which it is cut off. Place that part in water and it should take about 3 months to properly grow out its roots. After, you can place these propagation in a mini pot of the appropriate soil for the plant. Make sure the pot in not too deep otherwise your new cutting might get root rot. Not all propagation is successful, but you can always try it again once your old plant has new growth.

New plant owner?

Here are some of the most beginner friendly plants, March 3, 2021

The Rubber plant is one of the most easiest house plant to care for. It virtually need no water because it grows in dry environment naturally so it can be left alone for a while. In the wild, this plant can grow into incredible heights, but in a pot you can have your own mini tree. Another easy plant, would have to be Snake plant because of its ability to withstand neglect. This plant can be place in offices with little light yet still able to survive, due to the hardiness of the plant. Last easy plant would be, Zz plant is another that can survive neglect and little watering. The plant grows naturally in dry environment, and is able to withstand no attention from the plantowner. With little watering, the plant is still able to throw out green leaves. Hope these plants can help you choose your new beginner friendly plant.
